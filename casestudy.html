FileWeave: A social cloud storage solution. Keeping all realms of your digital life centralized and organized.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

FileWeave is a single cloud storage platform that keeps your personal, school, and professional files organized in one place. Staying connected with other users, FileWeave helps you stay connected through being able to easily collaborate and download editable files to enhance your workflow.

DISCOVERY

PROBLEM

Ever have issues keeping your cloud storage organized? Between work, family, and your personal files, it’s easy to get disorganized. When you’re working on multiple platforms to store and share personal and professional life documents, streamlined workflow is inhibited.

SOLUTION

FileWeave as a minimum viable product provides file storage, creation, and collaboration. With enhanced organizational features, and the capability to easily share and display your files for all realms of your life, FileWeave is the answer to your cloud storage needs.

RESEARCH

RESEARCH OVERVIEW

First off, I had to test the client's, and my own assumptions about cloud storage users needed to be confirmed or denied with research. Identifying use context and needs of potential users was done through deploying a user survey. With the initial survey responses, an empathetic understanding of how individuals use and think about cloud storage was gained.

PERSONAS

After the survey results were analyzed, I created user personas to key in on user needs and frustrations as I designed FileWeave.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

I did a SWOT analysis to learn more about the ecosystem of cloud storage platfoms FileWeave was entering. I researched the ease of document uploading, navigation, organization, effectiveness of UI and UX, and task facilitation and special features, and yielded these results:

Google Drive is the most popular service because of it’s integrated services. The organization is intuitive and collaboration is effective.

Weakness: Photo storage is separate, businesses feel Google Drive is insecrure. Social uses are limited to your inner circle, though there are downloadable documents available on the web, there is no feature to access them.

Google Drive is the most popular service because of it’s integrated services. The organization is intuitive and collaboration is effective.

Weakness: Photo storage is separate, businesses feel Google Drive is insecrure. Social uses are limited to your inner circle, though there are downloadable documents available on the web, there is no feature to access them.

Tresorit is a Swiss cloud storage platform emphasizing security. Businesses love to use tresorit because of it’s security features, and from the user surveys, we learned that secure file storage matters greatly to users.

Weakness: Doesn’t offer file creation or collaboration, and the UI makes it close to impossible to organize files. Once they are uploaded.

APPLYING THE RESULTS

1. All three platforms are tapered to a specific type of storage, but users have to use different platforms based on their user purpose. FileWeave can provide one centralized platform. 2. Dropbox lacks file creation, Tresorit lacks organization. FileWeave will allow you to store, edit and organize files. 3. GoogleDrive lacks access to public social documents.FileWeave will have a social feed where you can download public documents, which will be what it’s known for.

CONCLUSION

WHAT I LEARNED

Going through the empathetic process of research and design, I was able to successfully apply user feedback to discover direction for creating an effective solution to the problem at hand. Since this was my first big project, I went through major ups, downs and learning curves. Notabley, I began developing a mindful practice of personal bias elimination in the design and research process. Through multiple rounds of aggregating user data, the importance of testing and re-testing remarkably improved the quality of my work. From this experience, I have a deep appriciation and understanding of the critical nature of user testing. Having my work critiqued and tested over and over again was often stressful and disheartening, but now that I’ve grown a thick skin as a designer, I know that critique is the catalyst for growth in my designs.
