A little about me

At a young age, I took on the role of our family’s in-house tech support. Whenever my parents had an issue with technology, I was there to coach them through the task completion. Being witness to my parent's click patterns was often painful because they weren’t conditioned to understand non-readable cues, or common UI patterns that were second nature to me. Their frustration was continuous, along with my patience and empathy for their experience as non-native tech users. Today, after years of practice and interface innovation, they are both avid tech users who pull out their computers first thing in the morning.

It’s my goal and major motivation as a UX Designer to empower users through enriching their relationship with technology. The products people use dictate their human experience, and it’s my goal to make that experience a meaningful one. I see UX is a bridge towards individual empowerment, and I am the bridge builder.
