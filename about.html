A little about me

At a young age, I took on the role of our family’s in-house tech support. Whenever my parents had an issue with technology, I was there to coach them through the task completion. Being witness to my parent's click patterns was often painful because they weren’t conditioned to understand non-readable cues, or common UI patterns that were second nature to me. Their frustration was continuous, along with my patience and empathy for their experience as non-native tech users.Today, after years of practice and interface innovation, they are both avid tech users who pull out their computers first thing in the morning. As their tech-coach and only child, I continue to want them to reap maximum benefit from technology. It’s my goal and major motivation as a UX Designer to empower users through enriching their relationship with technology. The products people use dictate their human experience, and it’s my goal to make that experience a meaningful one. see UX is a bridge towards individual empowerment, and I am the bridge builder.

This is how I got here

  1. 2008

    In high school, I spent a lot of time making photos in the darkroom, and making graphic art on Indesign.

  2. 2010

    In college I studied how and why people do the things they do on a societal, cultural, and economic level.

  3. 2013

    I spent a quarter in China conducting qualitative research. Such as in-depth interviews and focus groups.

  4. 2014

    I spent this time on a solo adventure aroudn the world, where I satiated my need to explore and learn about how other people live their lives around the globe.

  5. 2015

    Unsure what career move to make, I surrounded myself in design via architecture. Here I was in charge of the company website which was a pivotal part of my path towards discovering UX.

  6. 2016

    With extremely strong people skills and an empathetic demeanor, I excelled at counseling families, but wasn't content. I needed a job where I could be creating.

  7. 2018

    Finding a career that combines my passion to create, and my strength of understanding people felt like everything I had been searching for in one. January 2018 I started my path towards a career in UX!
